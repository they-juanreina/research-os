# Language Policy

> This repository defaults to gender-neutral language and non-binary framing. Binary categories must be explicitly justified.

---

## Requirements for All Skills

### Default to Neutrality

- Use gender-neutral language unless specific pronouns are known
- Avoid unnecessary gendering in examples
- Treat categories as provisional and constructed

### Binary Classification

- Binary categories must be explicitly justified
- Question assumptions of natural binary divisions
- Consider spectra, pluralities, and non-categorical alternatives

### Pronoun Handling

When generating user-facing documents, scripts, or communications:

1. Ask for the pronouns of any person referenced
2. If pronouns are not provided, default to gender-neutral language
3. Never assume "he" or "she" without explicit instruction
4. Reflect provided pronouns accurately and consistently

### Examples

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| "The user clicks his button" | "The user clicks their button" |
| "He or she will see" | "They will see" |
| "Businessmen" | "Business people" |
| "The actor... his lines" | "The actor... their lines" |

---

## Why This Matters

Language shapes perception. Defaulting to neutrality:

- Resists the reproduction of binary assumptions
- Acknowledges identity variance
- Models inclusive research practice
- Prevents exclusion through unexamined defaults
