## PART 1 — Introduction & Setup
**⏱ Duration:** [X] min | **Elapsed:** 00:00–00:[XX] | **Clock:** [HH:MM]–[HH:MM]
**🎯 Covers:** Rapport, session framing, logistics

**💡 Rationale:** This section builds initial rapport and gives the participant a mental map of the session before anything is asked of them. Participants who understand the format are less anxious and more forthcoming throughout.

---

> "Good [morning/afternoon] — and thank you so much for making time today. I really appreciate it.
>
> My name is [FACILITATOR NAME], and I'm a researcher working with the [TEAM NAME] team. With me today — quietly in the background — is [NOTE-TAKER NAME], who'll be taking notes as we go.
>
> We're going to spend about [DURATION] together. We'll start with a few questions about how you work day to day, and then — a bit later on — I'll show you something and get your reaction to it. There's nothing to prepare or study — I'm just here to listen and learn from you.
>
> Before we get into it, I have a few housekeeping things to cover."

## PART 2 — Consent & Ground Rules
**⏱ Duration:** [X] min | **Elapsed:** 00:[XX]–00:[XX] | **Clock:** [HH:MM]–[HH:MM]
**🎯 Covers:** Recording consent, ground rules, confidentiality, anonymity, silence note, opening for questions

**💡 Rationale:** Establishing consent and ground rules early creates psychological safety. Participants who understand they are evaluating designs — not being evaluated themselves — give more honest, critical feedback. The silence note prevents an awkward dynamic when the note-taker is typing.

---

> "First — I'd like to ask your permission to record our session today. That would include the audio of our conversation, and video if you're comfortable with that. If we share a screen at any point, that would be recorded too.
>
> The recording stays within our research team — it helps us go back and check details rather than relying on memory alone. Nothing is shared externally.
>
> Is that alright with you?"

**[WAIT FOR RESPONSE]**

> ⚑ IF YES: **[START RECORDING]** — then continue.
> ⚑ IF NO: Thank them. Note that session notes only will be used. Continue without recording.

📋 NOTE-TAKER: Note consent decision in session metadata.

> "Thank you. And just so you know — everything you share today is confidential. We may want to reference quotes in our reports, but we'd always use your first name only, unless you'd prefer to remain completely anonymous. I'll ask you again at the end, so there's no pressure to decide now.
>
> Now — a few things that I think will help us both get the most out of this.
>
> The first is: please be honest, even about things that might feel uncomfortable to say. If something looks confusing, broken, or just doesn't make sense to you — that's exactly what we need to hear. We're evaluating the designs, not you. So if something doesn't work, please say so — you won't hurt anyone's feelings.
>
> Second — if anything I ask is unclear, or if a question doesn't quite apply to your situation, please just tell me. I'd rather rephrase than have you answer something that doesn't fit.
>
> Third — you can ask me questions at any point. I might not always be able to answer — sometimes we want to see what the design does on its own — but I'll do my best.
>
> And fourth — you might notice some silence on my end while [NOTE-TAKER NAME] or I are writing something down. Please don't worry about that — it usually means something you said was worth capturing. Silence is completely normal in these sessions."

→ PROBE: "Does that all make sense?"

> "Is there anything you'd like to ask me before we get started?"

**[WAIT — answer any questions briefly and without leading. If they ask about the project: "We're exploring some new features — I'll tell you more at the end."]**

> "Perfect — let's go."

**[TRANSITION TO WARM-UP]**

## PART [N+1] — Debrief & Hypothesis Validation
**⏱ Duration:** [X] min | **Elapsed:** 00:[XX]–00:[XX] | **Clock:** [HH:MM]–[HH:MM]
**🎯 Covers:** [Hypothesis being tested, strategic questions, value probing]

**💡 Rationale:** The debrief steps back from specific tasks to test broader hypotheses about value and mental model. It is the most frequently cut section — protect it by finishing tasks on time. The hypothesis question should be asked as a scenario, not as "What do you think of this feature?"

## PART [N+2] — Close
**⏱ Duration:** [X] min | **Elapsed:** 00:[XX]–00:[XX] | **Clock:** [HH:MM]–[HH:MM]
**🎯 Covers:** Open floor, anonymity confirmation, next steps

**💡 Rationale:** Closing on a positive, open note gives participants space to share anything they held back. Confirming anonymity preference at the end — rather than only at the start — respects that people sometimes change their minds after speaking.

---

> "Before we wrap up — is there anything you noticed today, or anything we talked about, that you'd like to come back to or add to?"

**[WAIT FOR RESPONSE]**

> "And — one last thing. About the quotes we may have taken today. Everything is confidential and used only internally. When we write up our findings, we sometimes include short quotes to bring the data to life. Would you be comfortable with us attributing a quote to your first name? Or would you prefer to remain anonymous? Either is completely fine."

📋 NOTE-TAKER: Record anonymity preference in session metadata.
